Why It Works: Verification-First Principle

üéØ Core Insight: Garbage In = Garbage Out

Traditional AI workflows start with indexing, which allows models to confidently hallucinate "facts" that then propagate through the entire pipeline. By verifying first with Perplexity's real-time search capability, we establish a foundation of verified truth that prevents all downstream hallucination.

The secret lies in how we engineered the Perplexity Step 0 prompt:

  1. Focus on 6 critical fields only ‚Äî Parent company, official website, Instagram account, product category, specific product type, and launch year. Requesting fewer fields dramatically reduces hallucination risk.
  2. Use country-specific search terms ‚Äî "Kata Oma Indonesia produsen" finds the correct Indonesian brand, preventing confusion with similarly-named companies in other countries.
  3. Demand source verification ‚Äî Perplexity must cite Indonesian business news, official company registrations, and verified press releases.
  4. Accept uncertainty explicitly ‚Äî Better to return "Unable to verify" than to hallucinate with false confidence.
